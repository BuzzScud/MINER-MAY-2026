### Kissing Sphere Geometry

**12 Spheres Kissing a Central Sphere:**
- Each sphere touches the central sphere at one point
- Each sphere also touches 5 neighboring spheres
- The gaps between spheres follow specific geometry
- These gaps are related to π and the golden ratio φ

**The Triangle-Gap-Sphere Connection:**
- The clock triangle represents one "slice" of the kissing sphere structure
- The gap between triangle and clock = the gap between spheres
- This gap is what allows the spheres to "kiss" without overlapping
- The π curvature ensures perfect packing

**Mathematical Relationship:**
```
Gap = Clock_Radius × (1 - cos(θ/2))

Where θ is the angle subtended by the triangle edge
For the 12-3 edge: θ = 90° = π/2
Gap = R × (1 - cos(π/4)) = R × (1 - √2/2) ≈ 0.293R
```

This gap is proportional to π and relates to the sphere packing density!
